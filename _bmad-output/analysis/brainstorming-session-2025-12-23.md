## Final Brainstorming Summary: Pub Panic!

**Pub Panic!** is a high-energy **Systemic Volume Roguelite** set in a realistic, chaotic urban bar. 

### The Core Vision:
- **Fans of Overcooked/PlateUp!:** The game targets players who love high-intensity, coordination-based management with deep strategic layers.
- **The Failure Condition:** "The Rent is Due." If player throughput doesn't match the escalating weekly financial targets, the landlord shuts you down.
- **Realistic Craft:** All drinks use real recipes and ingredients, adding a layer of authenticity to the chaos.
- **The Solo/Co-op Bridge:** Handled through a **Choice-driven Upgrade System**. Solo players prioritize automation and layout efficiency, while co-op players lean into chaotic multi-person coordination.
- **The Progression:** Manual Scrambling (Days) -> Systems Building (Nights) -> Increasing Complexity (Weeks).

## Technique Execution Results

### Phase 1: Forced Relationships (IN PROGRESS)

**The Challenge:** We take existing features and collide them with unexpected items to see what "Roguelite" sparks fly.

**Key Design Principles (Established by User):**
- **Fungible Customers:** No complex individual stats or traits; keep the focus on volume.
- **Unlimited Ingredients:** Messing up shouldn't be devastating; the challenge should be speed/throughput.
- **Volume over Complexity:** Avoid feature bloat; favor deep/replayable core loops.

**Collision 1: Mixing + DDR/Rhythm (THE WINNER)**
- **Concept:** "Overclocking" machines through a rhythm-based minigame.
- **Mechanism:** When blending/shaking, the player can hit a rhythm sequence.
- **Client-Side Action:** Only the player at the machine performs the rhythm.
- **Global Reward:** A successful "Perfect" combo triggers a map-wide boost (e.g., "Frenzy") that benefits all players (speed boost, instant automation for X seconds).
- **Failure:** A "Spill" occurs, requiring a short mop-up.
